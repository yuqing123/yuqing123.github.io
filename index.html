Explore how many young adults live with their parents

More and more young adults (18 to 34) now live with their parents, according to the U.S. Census Bureau. The number jumped from 23% in 1960 to 31% in recent data. The increase has raised important questions regarding household formation and its impact on owning, renting, and the demand for new housing construction.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Decennial Censuses, 1960 to 1980, and Current Population Survey. (link)
              Annual Social and Economic Supplements, 1983 to 2014.
